ðŸ”„ Multi-Planar Motion

Surfing demands exceptional movement competency across all three planes of motion, with particular emphasis on transverse (rotational) power and frontal plane stability. The sport requires rapid transitions between planes during maneuvers, creating unique demands for multi-directional stability and power output. Research by Furness et al. (2016) demonstrates that elite surfers exhibit 18-22% greater rotational power and 15% better frontal plane stability compared to recreational surfers, highlighting the importance of multi-planar training.

Scientific Foundation: Dr. Stuart McGill - The Spine Specialist

Dr. McGill's research on spine biomechanics and core stability has revolutionized our understanding of rotational power development. His studies demonstrate that optimal rotational power comes from a stable core that transfers force rather than creating it through spinal movement. This is particularly relevant for surfers who must generate powerful rotational movements while maintaining spinal integrity on unstable surfaces.

Research Application: "Core training protocols implement McGill's principles of 'proximal stiffness for distal mobility,' developing a stable spine that efficiently transfers force while protecting against the high rotational loads experienced during aggressive surfing maneuvers."

Planes of movement required:

  • Sagittal: Pop-up mechanics, paddling, duck diving
  • Frontal: Rail-to-rail transitions, bottom turns, stability in lateral swell
  • Transverse: Cutbacks, rotational maneuvers, carving turns

Surfing requires a unique combination of force production capabilities: explosive power for maneuvers and pop-ups, isometric endurance for paddling, and rapid stabilization in unpredictable environments. Research by Tran et al. (2015) demonstrates that elite surfers produce peak power outputs of 6.9-8.5 W/kg during explosive pop-ups and 5.3-6.8 W/kg during rotational maneuvers. The sport demands rapid force development (RFD) with critical time windows of 150-300ms to execute technical maneuvers on dynamic wave faces.

Scientific Foundation: Dr. Vladimir Zatsiorsky - Strength Science Pioneer

Dr. Zatsiorsky's research on rate of force development (RFD) and explosive strength has transformed our understanding of power training. His work distinguishes between maximum strength and explosive strength, demonstrating that specific training methods are required to develop each quality. For surfers, the ability to generate force quickly (RFD) is often more important than absolute strength.

Research Application: "Power development protocols are designed using Zatsiorsky's principles of explosive strength training, emphasizing rate of force development through ballistic training methods that match the 150-300ms force production windows required for critical surfing maneuvers."

Type of force needed:

  • Explosive power with rapid stabilization (pop-ups, aerial maneuvers)
  • Rate of force development: Critical for maneuver execution (150-300ms windows)
  • Force application direction: Multi-directional with emphasis on rotational power

Day 1: Rotational Power Development

Warm-up: Surf-Specific Movement Preparation (15 minutes)

1. Quadruped Thoracic Rotations

Execution: From hands and knees position, place one hand behind head. Rotate elbow toward opposite knee, then rotate upward toward ceiling, following with eyes. Focus on isolating thoracic spine movement.

Sets/Reps: 2 sets of 10 per side

Equipment: Exercise mat

Scientific Basis: Targets thoracic mobility identified by Lundgren et al. (2018) as critical for rotational surfing maneuvers

2. Banded Hip Rotations

Execution: Secure band around knees in athletic stance. Maintain stable upper body while rotating knees inward and outward against band resistance, focusing on controlled movement.

Sets/Reps: 2 sets of 15 each direction

Equipment: Medium resistance band

Scientific Basis: Addresses hip rotational mobility requirements documented by Donti et al. (2021)

3. Dynamic Ankle Mobilization

Execution: In lunge position, drive front knee forward while keeping heel down. Add gentle rotation of torso over front leg. Progressively increase range.

Sets/Reps: 2 sets of 10 per side

Equipment: None

Scientific Basis: Targets ankle dorsiflexion requirements (>35Â°) identified as critical for bottom turns

Main Workout: Rotational Power Circuit

1. Banded Rotational Chops

Execution: Anchor resistance band at shoulder height. Stand perpendicular to anchor point. Grasp band with both hands, rotate away from anchor through athletic stance, driving movement from ground up through hips.

Sets/Reps: 4 sets of 8 per side

Rest: 60 seconds between sets

Equipment: Heavy resistance band

Substitution: Kettlebell or dumbbell rotational chop if available

Scientific Basis: Implements McGill's research on rotational power development through proximal stability

Technical Cues: "Drive from ground," "Rotate through hips," "Maintain tall spine"

2. Explosive Push-up to Rotation

Execution: From push-up position, perform explosive push-up, then rotate body to side plank position with arm extended upward. Return to push-up position and repeat on opposite side.

Sets/Reps: 4 sets of 6 per side

Rest: 90 seconds between sets

Equipment: Exercise mat

Regression: Perform from knees if needed

Scientific Basis: Simulates pop-up to rotational maneuver sequence with Zatsiorsky's explosive strength principles

Technical Cues: "Explosive press," "Control rotation," "Stack shoulders in side position"

3. Stability Ball Jackknife with Rotation

Execution: Start in push-up position with feet on stability ball. Draw knees toward chest while rotating hips to one side. Return to start position and alternate sides.

Sets/Reps: 3 sets of 8 per side

Rest: 60 seconds between sets

Equipment: Stability ball

Substitution: Sliding towel on smooth floor if no ball available

Scientific Basis: Integrates core stability with rotational movement patterns as per McGill's research

Technical Cues: "Brace core throughout," "Control the rotation," "Keep shoulders stable"

Energy System Development: Wave Simulation Intervals

Execution: Complete 5 rounds of the following sequence with minimal transition time:

  1. 30 seconds fast-paced mountain climbers (simulates paddling)
  2. Explosive pop-up to surf stance (simulates catching wave)
  3. 30 seconds surf stance isometric hold with upper body rotations (simulates riding wave)
  4. 5 burpees (simulates wipeout and paddle back out)

Rest: 90 seconds between rounds

Equipment: Exercise mat

Scientific Basis: Matches energy system demands identified by Farley et al. (2012) in competitive surfing analysis

Technical Cues: "Explosive transitions," "Stable surf stance," "Full commitment to each movement"

Technical Focus: Rotational Mechanics

During this session, focus on these specific technical elements:

Cool-down: Targeted Mobility (10 minutes)

1. Thoracic Foam Rolling

Execution: Place foam roller perpendicular to spine at mid-back. Support head with hands, lift hips slightly, and roll from mid to upper back. Add gentle extensions over roller at tender spots.

Duration: 2 minutes

Equipment: Foam roller

Day 2: Surf-Specific Strength & Stability

Warm-up: Neural Activation Circuit (12 minutes)

Complete 2 rounds of the following circuit:

1. Band Pull-Aparts with External Rotation

Execution: Hold band at shoulder width. Pull band apart while adding external rotation at end range, bringing hands toward upper back.

Reps: 15

Equipment: Light resistance band

2. Quadruped Rocking with Hip Rotation

Execution: From hands and knees, rock backward toward heels while rotating one hip outward. Return to start and alternate sides.

Reps: 10 per side

Equipment: Exercise mat

3. Active Spiderman Lunge with Rotation

Execution: From push-up position, step one foot to outside of hand. Rotate torso upward, reaching hand to ceiling. Return and repeat on opposite side.

Reps: 8 per side

Equipment: Exercise mat

Main Workout: Surf-Specific Strength Circuit

1. TRX Suspended Push-up to Pop-up

Execution: Perform push-up with feet in TRX straps. At top of push-up, explosively bring one foot forward into surf stance position. Return to start and alternate lead foot.

Sets/Reps: 4 sets of 6 per side

Rest: 90 seconds between sets

Equipment: TRX suspension trainer

Substitution: Push-up to surf stance on floor if no TRX available

Scientific Basis: Simulates pop-up mechanics while adding instability element

Technical Cues: "Explosive transition," "Stable landing," "Athletic stance"

2. Single-Leg RDL with Band Row

Execution: Stand on one leg holding band anchored in front. Hinge at hip while extending free leg behind for counterbalance. At bottom of hinge, perform rowing motion with both arms. Return to standing.

Sets/Reps: 3 sets of 10 per side

Rest: 60 seconds between sets

Equipment: Medium resistance band

Substitution: Use household items for weight if no band available

Scientific Basis: Develops single-leg stability identified by Furness et al. (2016) as critical for elite surfers

Technical Cues: "Hinge, don't squat," "Stable hip," "Squeeze shoulder blades"

3. Stability Ball Body Saw

Execution: Assume forearm plank position with forearms on ground and feet on stability ball. Using core control, saw body forward and backward while maintaining rigid plank position.

Sets/Reps: 3 sets of 45 seconds

Rest: 60 seconds between sets

Equipment: Stability ball

Substitution: Forearm plank with feet on towel on smooth floor

Scientific Basis: Implements McGill's research on dynamic core stability

Technical Cues: "Rigid body line," "Breathe throughout," "Control the movement"

Progression Plan

Scientific Foundation: Dr. Brad Schoenfeld - The Hypertrophy Expert

While Dr. Schoenfeld is known for hypertrophy research, his work on training variables (volume, intensity, frequency) has broad applications for all training goals. His research demonstrates that systematic progression of these variables is essential for continued adaptation, regardless of the specific training outcome.

Research Application: "Progressive overload is implemented using Schoenfeld's evidence-based principles, systematically increasing training stress through volume, intensity, and complexity variables to drive continued adaptation."

Periodized approach with competition-specific peaking. Track rotational power output and technical efficiency as primary metrics. Implement a 3-week loading cycle followed by a 1-week deload to optimize recovery and adaptation.

Weekly Progression

  • Week 1-3: Volume phase - Emphasize technical refinement and volume accumulation
  • Week 4: Deload - Reduce volume by 40%, maintain intensity
  • Week 5-7: Intensity phase - Increase intensity, reduce volume
  • Week 8: Taper - Reduce volume by 60%, maintain intensity
  • Week 9: Competition week - Minimal volume, strategic activation sessions

Nutrition Focus

Periodized nutrition approach aligned with training phases. For a competitive surfer, focus on:

  • Training day nutrition:
    • Pre-training: 1-2g/kg carbohydrates, 0.3g/kg protein, 3-4 hours before
    • Intra-training: 30-60g/hour carbohydrates for sessions >90 minutes
    • Post-training: 0.3g/kg protein + 0.8g/kg carbohydrates within 30 minutes
  • Daily macronutrient targets:
    • Protein: 1.8-2.2g/kg of bodyweight, evenly distributed
    • Carbohydrates: 5-7g/kg on training days, 3-5g/kg on rest days
    • Fats: 0.8-1.2g/kg from quality sources
  • Hydration: 1oz per pound of bodyweight plus 16-20oz per hour of training
  • Supplementation: Creatine monohydrate (5g daily), vitamin D3 (2000-5000 IU), magnesium (300-400mg)
