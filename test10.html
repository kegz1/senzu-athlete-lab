üåä DAY 1 ‚Äì PADDLE POWER & SHOULDER ENDURANCE

Scientific Foundation: Dr. Jeremy Sheppard - Surf Performance Researcher

Dr. Sheppard's research with elite surfers at the Australian Institute of Sport demonstrated that upper body pulling power has the highest correlation with competitive surfing performance. His studies show that elite surfers generate 25-30% more force during paddling than recreational surfers, with specific emphasis on the latissimus dorsi, posterior deltoids, and serratus anterior muscles.

Research Application: "Paddle power training implements Sheppard's research on force production patterns, developing the specific upper body pulling strength and endurance required for efficient paddling in variable surf conditions."

Warm-Up:

Arm Circles

Execution: Stand with feet shoulder-width apart. Extend arms to sides at shoulder height. Make small circles forward for 15 reps, then backward for 15 reps, gradually increasing circle size.

Sets/Reps: 30 total circles (15 forward, 15 backward)

Equipment: None

Scientific Basis: Activates rotator cuff muscles and increases synovial fluid in shoulder joint

Band Face Pulls

Execution: Anchor resistance band at shoulder height. Grasp band with both hands, palms down. Pull band toward face, separating hands and externally rotating shoulders at end range. Squeeze shoulder blades together.

Sets/Reps: 1 set of 15 reps

Equipment: Resistance band

Substitution: Use towel with isometric tension if no band available

Scientific Basis: Activates posterior deltoids and external rotators critical for shoulder health during paddling

Serratus Wall Slides

Execution: Stand facing wall with forearms on wall, elbows at shoulder height. Slide arms upward while pushing forearms into wall, focusing on serratus anterior activation (pushing shoulder blades apart).

Sets/Reps: 1 set of 10 reps

Equipment: Wall

Scientific Basis: Activates serratus anterior, critical for scapular stability during paddling motion

Main Workout:

Resistance Band Lat Pulldown

Execution: Anchor resistance band overhead. Kneel or sit with arms extended overhead grasping band. Pull band down to chest level, focusing on engaging lats and maintaining upright posture.

Sets/Reps: 3 sets of 15 reps

Rest: 60 seconds between sets

Equipment: Resistance band

Substitution: Prone Y raises if no band available

Scientific Basis: Targets latissimus dorsi, the primary muscle for paddling power as identified by Sheppard et al. (2013)

Technical Cues: "Pull with elbows," "Squeeze shoulder blades down and back," "Maintain tall posture"

Renegade Rows

Execution: Start in push-up position with hands on dumbbells or household items. Maintaining plank position, row one weight to hip while stabilizing with opposite arm. Lower and repeat on opposite side.

Sets/Reps: 3 sets of 12 reps (6 per side)

Rest: 60 seconds between sets

Equipment: Dumbbells or household items (water bottles, books)

Scientific Basis: Combines anti-rotation core stability with pulling strength, mimicking the stabilization demands of paddling through chop

Technical Cues: "Maintain rigid plank," "Squeeze shoulder blade at top," "Minimize hip rotation"

Prone Y-T-W Raises

Execution: Lie face down on mat or stability ball. Perform raises in Y position (arms overhead in Y shape), T position (arms straight out to sides), and W position (elbows bent at 90¬∞, upper arms at shoulder height).

Sets/Reps: 2 sets of 10 reps in each position

Rest: 45 seconds between sets

Equipment: Exercise mat or stability ball

Scientific Basis: Targets posterior shoulder muscles critical for paddling endurance and shoulder health

Technical Cues: "Initiate movement from shoulder blades," "Keep neck neutral," "Control the lowering phase"

Band Swimmers Pull

Execution: Anchor resistance band at chest height. Face band with arms extended. Perform alternating pulling motions mimicking swimming stroke, focusing on controlled tempo.

Sets/Duration: 3 sets of 20 seconds

Rest: 40 seconds between sets

Equipment: Resistance band

Substitution: Pantomime swimming motion with light weights or water bottles

Scientific Basis: Replicates the neuromuscular pattern of paddling with resistance

Technical Cues: "Full range of motion," "Control the tempo," "Engage core throughout"

Plank to Push-Up

Execution: Start in forearm plank position. One arm at a time, press up to hand, transitioning to push-up position. Lower back to forearm plank one arm at a time. Alternate leading arm.

Sets/Reps: 3 sets of 12 transitions (6 per side)

Rest: 60 seconds between sets

Equipment: Exercise mat

Scientific Basis: Develops shoulder stability and core control needed for pop-up mechanics

Technical Cues: "Maintain rigid plank," "Control the transition," "Keep hips stable"

Finisher:

Paddle Intervals (Land)

Execution: In prone position on exercise mat, perform maximum effort paddling motion for 30 seconds, followed by 30 seconds of slow, recovery paddling. Focus on full range of motion and power.

Sets/Duration: 5 rounds (30s hard / 30s slow)

Equipment: Exercise mat

Scientific Basis: Simulates the high-intensity paddling intervals required to catch waves and paddle through impact zones

Technical Cues: "Full extension," "High elbow recovery," "Powerful pull phase"

üßò DAY 2 ‚Äì SURF MOBILITY & CORE FLOW

Scientific Foundation: Dr. Kelly Starrett - Mobility Expert

Dr. Starrett's research on movement quality and joint position has revolutionized how we approach mobility for athletes. His work demonstrates that optimal performance requires not just range of motion, but control and stability throughout that range. For surfers, this integrated approach to mobility is essential for maintaining technique during dynamic movements on the wave face.

Research Application: "Mobility protocols implement Starrett's concept of 'mobility with stability,' developing not just range of motion but position awareness and motor control throughout that range‚Äîcritical for maintaining technique during dynamic surfing maneuvers."

Mobility Flow (2‚Äì3 rounds):

Archer Lunge + Twist

Execution: Step into lunge position. Rotate torso toward front leg while extending opposite arm as if drawing bow. Maintain stable lower body while rotating through thoracic spine.

Sets/Reps: 2-3 rounds of 10 reps per side

Equipment: None

Scientific Basis: Develops hip mobility and thoracic rotation needed for bottom turns and cutbacks

Technical Cues: "Rotate from mid-back," "Keep front knee stable," "Reach long through fingertips"

Cossack Squats

Execution: Stand with feet wider than shoulder-width. Shift weight to one leg, bending knee while keeping other leg straight. Lower as far as mobility allows, then return to center and repeat on opposite side.

Sets/Reps: 2-3 rounds of 8 reps per side

Equipment: None

Scientific Basis: Develops lateral hip mobility and ankle dorsiflexion needed for rail-to-rail transitions

Technical Cues: "Chest up," "Heel down on working leg," "Sit between hips"

Thoracic Opener

Execution: Lie on side with knees bent at 90¬∞. Extend bottom arm forward at shoulder height. Place top hand on top knee. Rotate top arm open toward ceiling, following with eyes. Return and repeat.

Sets/Reps: 2-3 rounds of 10 reps per side

Equipment: Exercise mat

Scientific Basis: Improves thoracic rotation critical for powerful turns and cutbacks

Technical Cues: "Initiate from mid-back," "Keep knees stacked," "Follow hand with eyes"

Down Dog to Up Dog Flow

Execution: Start in downward dog position. Flow forward to upward dog by lowering hips and pushing chest forward. Return to downward dog and repeat with fluid movement.

Sets/Reps: 2-3 rounds of 5 complete flows

Equipment: Exercise mat

Scientific Basis: Develops shoulder mobility and core control needed for paddling and pop-up mechanics

Technical Cues: "Long spine in both positions," "Active shoulders," "Fluid transition"

Prone Swimmer Stretch

Execution: Lie face down with arms extended overhead. Lift one arm and opposite leg, hold for 15 seconds. Lower and repeat with opposite arm/leg.

Sets/Duration: 2-3 rounds of 15-second holds per side

Equipment: Exercise mat

Scientific Basis: Improves extension pattern needed for paddling while developing posterior chain awareness

Technical Cues: "Reach long," "Keep neck neutral," "Maintain low-level activation"

Core Circuit:

Hollow Hold

Execution: Lie on back with arms extended overhead. Press lower back into floor while lifting shoulders and legs off ground. Maintain position with controlled breathing.

Sets/Duration: 3 sets of 20 seconds

Rest: 40 seconds between sets

Equipment: Exercise mat

Scientific Basis: Develops anterior core control needed for maintaining position on the wave face

Technical Cues: "Press back into floor," "Shoulders off mat," "Find tension, not pain"

Side Plank w/ Reach Under

Execution: Start in side plank position. Reach top arm under body with rotation, then return to extended position. Maintain stable hip position throughout.

Sets/Reps: 3 sets of 10 reps per side

Rest: 45 seconds between sets

Equipment: Exercise mat

Scientific Basis: Develops rotational control and lateral core stability needed for turns

Technical Cues: "Stable base," "Rotate through mid-back," "Control the motion"

V-Sit Rotations

Execution: Sit in V position with knees bent, feet off floor. Rotate torso side to side, touching hands to floor beside hips. Maintain elevated chest and feet throughout.

Sets/Reps: 3 sets of 12 rotations (6 per side)

Rest: 45 seconds between sets

Equipment: Exercise mat

Scientific Basis: Develops rotational power and core control needed for cutbacks and snaps

Technical Cues: "Tall spine," "Initiate from core," "Control the tempo"

Hanging Knee Raises (or Lying if no bar)

Execution: Hang from bar with shoulders engaged. Raise knees to chest with control, then lower without swinging. If no bar available, perform lying leg raises.

Sets/Reps: 3 sets of 10 reps

Rest: 60 seconds between sets

Equipment: Pull-up bar or exercise mat

Substitution: Lying leg raises if no bar available

Scientific Basis: Develops lower core control needed for leg drive during turns

Technical Cues: "Initiate with lower abs," "Control the descent," "Maintain shoulder engagement"

üèÑ DAY 3 ‚Äì LOWER BODY STRENGTH + POP-UP POWER

Scientific Foundation: Dr. Sophia Nimphius - Strength & Power Researcher

Dr. Nimphius's research on lower body power development has demonstrated that unilateral (single-leg) strength has a stronger correlation with change-of-direction performance than bilateral strength. Her work with elite surfers shows that the ability to rapidly produce force through a single leg is critical for generating speed and power during bottom turns and cutbacks.

Research Application: "Lower body training implements Nimphius's research on unilateral force production, developing the single-leg strength and power needed for generating drive through turns and maintaining stability on uneven wave faces."

Warm-Up:

Hip 90/90 Flow

Execution: Sit with one leg bent at 90¬∞ in front, other leg bent at 90¬∞ to side. Rotate between positions while maintaining tall spine. Flow between positions with control.

Sets/Reps: 5-8 transitions per side

Equipment: Exercise mat

Scientific Basis: Develops hip mobility in multiple planes needed for stance transitions

Glute Bridge March

Execution: Lie on back with knees bent, feet flat. Lift hips to bridge position. Maintaining hip height, lift one foot off ground, then return and repeat with opposite foot.

Sets/Reps: 10 total marches (5 per leg)

Equipment: Exercise mat

Scientific Basis: Activates glutes and develops hip stability needed for single-leg balance

Band Lateral Walks

Execution: Place mini-band around legs just above knees. Assume athletic stance with tension on band. Step sideways while maintaining tension, then return in opposite direction.

Sets/Reps: 20 steps total (10 per direction)

Equipment: Mini resistance band

Substitution: Lateral steps with isometric hold if no band available

Scientific Basis: Activates hip abductors needed for lateral stability during turns

Main Strength:

Bulgarian Split Squat

Execution: Place rear foot on bench or elevated surface. Lower into split squat position, keeping front knee aligned with toes. Drive through front foot to return to start position.

Sets/Reps: 3 sets of 8 reps per side

Rest: 60 seconds between sets

Equipment: Bench or chair, optional dumbbells for added resistance

Scientific Basis: Develops unilateral leg strength needed for generating drive through turns

Technical Cues: "Vertical shin," "Chest up," "Drive through midfoot"

Trap Bar Deadlift or DB RDL

Execution: For DB RDL: Hold weights at sides, hinge at hips while maintaining slight knee bend and flat back. Lower weights toward floor, then return to standing by driving hips forward.

Sets/Reps: 3 sets of 5 reps

Rest: 90 seconds between sets

Equipment: Dumbbells or household items

Scientific Basis: Develops posterior chain strength needed for generating power in turns

Technical Cues: "Hinge, don't squat," "Maintain neutral spine," "Drive hips forward"

Lateral Step-Up to Knee Drive

Execution: Stand beside bench or step. Step up laterally, then drive opposite knee up toward chest. Lower with control and repeat. Complete all reps on one side before switching.

Sets/Reps: 3 sets of 10 reps per side

Rest: 60 seconds between sets

Equipment: Bench, step, or sturdy elevated surface

Scientific Basis: Develops lateral power and single-leg stability needed for rail-to-rail transitions

Technical Cues: "Push through heel," "Tall posture," "Control the descent"

Depth Drop to Jump

Execution: Stand on low step or platform. Step off and immediately jump upward upon landing. Focus on minimal ground contact time and maximal height.

Sets/Reps: 3 sets of 5 reps

Rest: 90 seconds between sets

Equipment: Low step or platform (6-12 inches)

Substitution: Squat jumps if no platform available

Scientific Basis: Develops reactive strength and rate of force development needed for explosive maneuvers

Technical Cues: "Soft landing," "Explode up," "Minimal ground contact"

Finisher:

Pop-Up Repeats

Execution: Start in prone paddling position. Explosively push up and bring feet forward into surf stance in one fluid motion. Return to prone position and repeat.

Sets/Reps: 3 sets of 10 reps

Rest: 60 seconds between sets

Equipment: Exercise mat

Scientific Basis: Develops the specific movement pattern and explosive power needed for effective pop-ups

Technical Cues: "Explosive push," "Fast feet," "Land in athletic stance"

Focus: Form, explosiveness, and fast hands
