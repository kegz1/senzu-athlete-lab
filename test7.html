SENZU ATHLETE LAB - Detailed Exercise Breakdown

3-Day Powerlifting Training Plan for Intermediate - Strength Building

Day 1: Strength & Movement Development

Warm-up: Dynamic Mobility (10-15 minutes)

1. Hip Hinge Drill with Dowel

Execution: Place a dowel or PVC pipe along your spine, maintaining three points of contact (head, upper back, sacrum). Hinge at the hips while keeping a neutral spine, pushing hips back until you feel a stretch in hamstrings.

Sets/Reps: 2 sets of 10 reps

Purpose: Teaches proper hip hinge pattern for deadlifts and reinforces neutral spine position

2. Quadruped Thoracic Rotations

Execution: Start on hands and knees. Place one hand behind your head, rotate that elbow toward the opposite knee, then rotate upward toward the ceiling, following with your eyes.

Sets/Reps: 2 sets of 8 reps per side

Purpose: Improves thoracic mobility needed for proper squat and bench press positioning

3. Banded Shoulder Dislocates

Execution: Hold a resistance band with hands wider than shoulder-width. Keeping arms straight, bring the band from in front of your body, over your head, and behind your back, then reverse.

Sets/Reps: 2 sets of 10 reps

Purpose: Improves shoulder mobility and stability for bench press and overhead movements

4. Ankle Mobility Drill

Execution: In a half-kneeling position, place front foot 4-5 inches from wall. Drive knee forward to touch wall while keeping heel down. Progressively move foot back as mobility improves.

Sets/Reps: 2 sets of 10 reps per ankle

Purpose: Improves ankle dorsiflexion needed for proper squat depth

Main Workout

1. Front Squat

Execution: Position barbell on front deltoids with elbows high, creating a "shelf" with shoulders. Descend to below parallel while maintaining upright torso. Drive through midfoot, keeping elbows high throughout movement.

Sets/Reps/Intensity: 4 sets of 6-8 reps at 75-80% 1RM

Rest: 2 minutes between sets

Technical Cues: "Elbows high," "Chest up," "Knees track over toes," "Breathe into belt"

2. Overhead Pressing

Execution: Start with barbell at shoulder height, hands just outside shoulders. Brace core and press bar directly overhead until arms are fully extended. Lower under control to starting position.

Sets/Reps/Intensity: 4 sets of 8-10 reps at 70-75% 1RM

Rest: 90 seconds between sets

Technical Cues: "Tight core," "Press through the center line," "Full lockout at top," "Squeeze glutes"

3. Core Stability: Pallof Press

Execution: Attach band to rack at chest height. Stand perpendicular to anchor point, holding band at chest. Press band straight out and hold, resisting rotation. Return to chest and repeat.

Sets/Duration: 3 sets of 30-45 seconds per side

Rest: 60 seconds between sets

Technical Cues: "Resist rotation," "Brace core," "Maintain neutral spine," "Breathe throughout"

Technical Focus: Sport-specific Technique Development

During this session, focus on these specific technical elements:

  • Bracing Sequence: Practice proper bracing sequence for powerlifting - big breath into diaphragm, brace core 360째 against belt, maintain pressure throughout rep
  • Bar Path Analysis: Use video recording to analyze vertical bar path in squats and presses, making adjustments for optimal leverage
  • Foot Pressure: Practice "screwing feet into floor" during squats to create external rotation torque at hips

Cool-down: Targeted Mobility Work (5-10 minutes)

1. 90/90 Hip Stretch

Execution: Sit with one leg bent at 90째 in front, other leg bent at 90째 to the side. Keep spine tall and rotate torso toward front leg, feeling stretch in hip. Hold, then switch sides.

Duration: 1-2 minutes per side

Purpose: Releases hip rotators and improves hip mobility for squat and deadlift

2. Lat/Thoracic Foam Rolling

Execution: Place foam roller under upper back, perpendicular to spine. Support head with hands, lift hips slightly, and roll from mid to upper back. For lats, lie on side with roller under armpit, roll from armpit to mid-ribs.

Duration: 1-2 minutes per area

Purpose: Releases tension in lats and thoracic spine to improve overhead positioning

Day 2: Sport-Specific Development

Warm-up: Sport-specific Activation Drills (10-15 minutes)

1. Banded Good Mornings

Execution: Place light resistance band around shoulders, anchored under feet. Hinge at hips with soft knees, maintaining neutral spine. Push hips back until feeling hamstring stretch, then return to start.

Sets/Reps: 2 sets of 15 reps

Purpose: Activates posterior chain and reinforces hip hinge pattern

2. Banded Pull-Aparts

Execution: Hold light resistance band at shoulder width. Pull band apart by squeezing shoulder blades together, keeping arms straight. Control return to starting position.

Sets/Reps: 2 sets of 15 reps

Purpose: Activates upper back muscles needed for bench press stability

3. Glute Bridges with Band

Execution: Place mini-band just above knees. Lie on back with feet flat, knees bent. Drive through heels to lift hips, squeezing glutes at top. Maintain tension on band throughout.

Sets/Reps: 2 sets of 15 reps

Purpose: Activates glutes and reinforces hip extension pattern

Main Workout: Sport-specific Skill Development

1. Technical Drill: Pause Squats

Execution: Perform back squat with 3-second pause at bottom position. Maintain tight core and proper position during pause. Drive up explosively after pause.

Sets/Reps/Intensity: 4 sets of 5 reps at 65-70% 1RM

Rest: Full recovery (2-3 minutes)

Technical Cues: "Stay tight in the hole," "Maintain position," "Explode after pause"

2. Technical Drill: Tempo Bench Press

Execution: Perform bench press with 3-second eccentric (lowering) phase, 1-second pause at chest, then normal concentric. Focus on controlled descent and position at chest.

Sets/Reps/Intensity: 4 sets of 6 reps at 65-70% 1RM

Rest: Full recovery (2-3 minutes)

Technical Cues: "Control the descent," "Maintain tension," "Drive with leg drive"

3. Sport-specific Movement Pattern: Block Pulls

Execution: Perform deadlift from 2-4 inch blocks to emphasize lockout strength. Focus on hip drive and glute engagement at top of movement.

Sets/Reps/Intensity: 3 sets of 10-12 reps at 60-65% 1RM

Rest: 60 seconds between sets

Technical Cues: "Drive hips through," "Squeeze glutes at top," "Maintain neutral spine"

Energy System Training: Sport-specific Conditioning

1. Prowler Push/Sled Drag Circuit

Execution: Alternate between prowler push (30 meters) and backward sled drag (30 meters) with minimal rest between movements.

Sets/Duration: 4 rounds with 2 minutes rest between rounds

Purpose: Develops work capacity specific to powerlifting without compromising recovery

Technical Focus: Proper Form and Movement Patterns

During this session, focus on these specific technical elements:

  • Position Consistency: Maintain identical setup positions across all sets to build motor pattern consistency
  • Breathing Mechanics: Practice proper breathing mechanics - full diaphragmatic breath, brace, perform rep, controlled exhale at completion
  • Tension Management: Practice creating and maintaining whole-body tension throughout movements

Cool-down: Mobility Exercises for Injury Prevention

1. Half-Kneeling Hip Flexor Stretch with Rotation

Execution: In half-kneeling position, posteriorly tilt pelvis to stretch hip flexor of rear leg. Add gentle rotation toward front leg to increase stretch.

Duration: 1-2 minutes per side

Purpose: Releases hip flexors to improve hip extension in deadlifts and squats

2. Supine Pec Stretch with Foam Roller

Execution: Lie on foam roller positioned along spine. Extend arms out to sides at shoulder height with elbows bent at 90째. Allow gravity to open chest and shoulders.

Duration: 1-2 minutes

Purpose: Releases chest tightness to improve shoulder positioning in bench press

Day 3: Recovery & Supplementary Work

Active Recovery (20-30 minutes)

1. Light Circuit Training

Execution: Perform circuit of rowing machine (3 min), light medicine ball exercises (3 min), and walking (3 min) for 2-3 rounds at conversational pace (RPE 4-5/10).

Intensity: Heart rate between 120-140 BPM

Purpose: Promotes blood flow and recovery without adding significant fatigue

Targeted Work for Injury Prevention

1. Mobility Work: Thoracic Extension on Foam Roller

Execution: Place foam roller horizontally under upper back, hands supporting head. Gently extend over roller, focusing on thoracic extension. Move roller to different segments of thoracic spine.

Sets/Duration: 2-3 sets of 30-45 seconds per position

Purpose: Improves thoracic mobility to reduce compensatory lumbar extension in overhead movements

2. Mobility Work: Shoulder Controlled Articular Rotations (CARs)

Execution: Standing with one arm extended, perform slow, controlled full-range shoulder circles with tension throughout movement. Maximize range in all directions.

Sets/Duration: 2-3 sets of 30-45 seconds per shoulder

Purpose: Improves shoulder mobility and joint health for pressing movements

3. Corrective Exercise: Face Pulls

Execution: Using rope attachment on cable machine at head height, pull rope toward face with external rotation at end range, emphasizing rear deltoids and rotator cuff.

Sets/Reps: 2 sets of 12 reps, focus on quality

Purpose: Strengthens posterior shoulder muscles to balance pressing volume

4. Corrective Exercise: Bird Dogs

Execution: From quadruped position, simultaneously extend opposite arm and leg while maintaining neutral spine. Hold briefly at end range, then return to start.

Sets/Reps: 2 sets of 10-12 reps per side, focus on quality

Purpose: Improves core stability and coordination for injury prevention

Joint Stability, Mobility Maintenance, and Recovery Optimization

1. Soft Tissue Work: Self-Myofascial Release

Execution: Use foam roller, lacrosse ball, or massage tool on key areas: glutes, hamstrings, quads, lats, and pecs. Spend 1-2 minutes per area, focusing on tender spots.

Duration: 10-15 minutes total

Purpose: Reduces muscle tension and improves tissue quality for better recovery

2. Recovery Protocol: Contrast Therapy

Execution: Alternate between hot shower/bath (2-3 minutes) and cold shower/bath (30-60 seconds) for 3-4 cycles, ending with cold.

Duration: 10-15 minutes total

Purpose: Promotes blood flow, reduces inflammation, and accelerates recovery
